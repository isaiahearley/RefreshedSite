"Hey there! My name is Isaiah and I'm an aspiring Software Engineer located in San Diego County!"

I've had several careers throughout the past 4 years which have helped me gain a variety of skills including customer service, project planning, troubleshooting hardware + software, and scripting. I started my career at palomar college where I was given the opportunity to participate in work-study and earn income while being in school full-time. Although my time with Palomar College lasted for 1 year I was able to get valuable skills in communication and staying organized at work. Afterwards I worked at Walmart as a sales associate and climbed to a variety of positions within the same store with my last position there being a merchandise reconcilation supervisor. After Walmart I transitioned my career into Information Technology and was able to grasp important concepts in both hardware and software troubleshooting. My time at Micro Center was spent assisting up to 20+ customers daily, creating tickets, closing tickets and keeping constant communication with various customers. This fast-paced environment prepared me for my next job with Science Applications International Corporation as a IT Service Desk Analyst. In this role I was given the privilege to support 600k+ Navy civilians, military and contractors with by providing IT software support on a variety of issues with software ranging from Citrix, Adobe and Microsoft 365 applications. I was also given the opportunity to assist callers in mission critical assignments that were crucial in the scope of NMCI. After my time with the service desk I was contacted by my current company; Information System Solutions, which offered my current position, "Windows System Administrator". In this role I've gained a tremendous amount of scripting knowledge, primarily with the PowerShell language, and have created scripts to automate our workflow. Some notable contributions I have made so far include:

Information System Solutions

San Diego, California
  • Developed a script to process unique and large datasets that finds the computer name, primary user, manufacturer, device model, and BIOS version of machines connected to the network, which later gives the option to output a document that can be used to present data and make better informed decisions.

RNG Hell

This project was created using C++ which helped me understand object-oriented programming principles including abstraction, polymorphism and the creation of various classes. The program is a small game using a console that when launched gives players the options to choose between 2 characters who go against a wild bear enemy with different abilities and items at their disposal. The program had built-in randomized calculations for damage critical effects, damage reduction, and damage over time until either the player or the bear died.

This project was built using HTML, CSS with a bit of JavaScript in about 2 weeks. This script has allowed me to mass automate a variety of solutions on the NIWC PAC network, greatly assisting System Administrators and reducing the amount of time a security vulnerability lingers.
